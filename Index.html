A Food Journey

The Why

During 2019, I got hungry, so I decided to take a culinary cruise around the DMV.

  • The Inn at Little Washington
  • Little Serow
  • Supra
  • Maydan
  • Dakiya

The Inn at Little Washington

Starting off with a bang, the first stop was The Inn at Little Washington. A nice drive outside of D.C. takes you to a rural part of Virginia where you stumble upon this hidden gem.

Try the french fries.

Little Serow

A Thai tasting room, with seven courses of delicious and bold flavors that will warm you up and cool you down.

The Milk-Tea & Coffee Ice Cream is a real winner.

Supra

Supra, a Georgian (the country) restaurant, represents "A traditional tablecloth characteristic of The Republic of Georgia".

For the carb lovers, try the khachapuri and khinkali.

Maydan

Middle Eastern cuisine

The lamb shoulder takes a team effort, and the flatbread teleports you to another world.

Dakiya

Ramen, Sapporo style. I like to add menma and an onesa egg to my bowl.

The gyoza is a nice warmup to the noodles.
